Chinatown

About

Chinatown has managed to integrate the past with the present where pockets of history remain along with more modern sights. Famiy-run goldsmiths, medicinal halls and teahouses ply their trades next to sleeker neighbourhoods such as hipster bars and lifestyle shops. All these squeezed into five-foot-ways, dingny alleys. Together with racouse street hawkers, these are the relics of Chinatown's past.

Food Haven

Foodies can look forward to the variety of food offered at Chinatown Food Street. Be sure to try 'char kway teow' (stir-fried noodles) and 'satay' (barbecued meat skewers) as you walk along the row of hawker stalls, shophouse restaurants and kiosks along Smith Street.

If you are looking for a trendier experience, chic restaurants and bars are located in Neil Road, Duxton Road and Keong Saik Road. Head on over to Club Street and Ann Siang Road on Friday and Saturday nights to join locals and expats for dinner and drinks.

So much to do

Visitors can look forward to shopping in Chinatown. Pick up souvenirs in Pagoda Street, 'bak kwa' (barbecued meat) in New Bridge Road or traditional Chinese medicine in South Bridge Road.

Visit one of the Hindu temples, mosques or Chinese temples that sit right next to each other in Chinatown, Sri Mariamman Temple and Jamae Mosque are on South Bridge Road, as is the Buddha Tooth Relic Temple.

You can also choose to spend a few nights in Chinatown. Stay at a boutique hotel in Duxton Road, Keong Saik Road and Ann Siang Road. For budget stays, try Trengganu Street, Mosque Street and Temple Street.

To top it all off, there is free Wi-Fi throughout Chinatown to keep you connected as you sightsee, shop and eat. Visitors can also choose to join a guided tour from Chinatown Visitors' Centre.
