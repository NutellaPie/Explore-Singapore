Kampong Glam

About

Kampong Glam's name orginated from the word 'kampong' (village in Malay) and the 'gelam' tree (or Paperbark Tree) that grew in the area. The area has an eclectic blend of history, culture and super-trendy lifestyle scene.

A Malay enclave

Kampong glam originated as a fishing village at the mouth of Rochor River. It was known for the 'gelam' tree which was used for the building of ships. In 1822, Sir Stamford Raffles allocated the area to the Malay Sultan Hussain Mohammed Shah and others in the Muslim community, including Arab and Bugis traders.

Abundance of Delights

Be sure to stop by the Malay Heritage Centre, where you can get to know Malay history and culture better.

For those looking to catch a bite, try out the many local delicacies such as nasi padang (steam rice sserved with various dishes) or Malay kuih ('cakes'). You can even find Middle Eastern, Japanese, Swedish and even Mexican fare here.

Visitors who wish to get some shopping done can head to colourful Haji Lane - a row of multi-label stores, quirky boutiques as well as hip bars and cafes. Traditional wares such as Persian carpets, kebaya dresses and handmade perfumes can also be picked up from Arab Street and Bussorah Street.
