Little India

About

What was once home to a racecourse, cattle herders and brick kilns has now been transformed into this historic district known as Little India. Despite the absence of the places and people it once housed, time stands still in the district as olden-day trades sit next to newer businesses: flower-garland vendors, modern eateries, boutique hotels, as well as arts group.

A rich history

Little India had mainly European residents in the 1840s due to the racecourse, where they met and mingled.

It was only when cattle trading took root, where Indian migrant workers were hired as traders, turning it into a mostly indian trade. Certain goods and services took off, and mosques and Hindu temples were built.

Vibrant culture

Little India remains as one of Singapore's most vibrant districts. Visitors will be able to explore the mis of Hindu and Chinese temples, mosques and churches as they walk down the streets. You will also get to taste of South Indian vegetarian food, North Indian tandoori dishes and local fare like roti prata (round pancakes). You can also try to spot brewers 'pull' hot milk tea, also known as teh tarik (pulled tea in Malay), a unique sight in Singapore. Shoppers can look forward to visiting the 24-hour Mustafa Centre which offers everything from electronics to groceries.
