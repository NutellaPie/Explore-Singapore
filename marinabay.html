Marina Bay

About

Positioned at the heart of Singapore, Marina Bay offers a slew of attractions one can visit in town. Such attractions include the Marina Bay Sands Hotel, Arts Science Museum and the Fullerton Bay Hotel. Do scroll down for more details on each attraction!

Marina Bay Sands Hotel

The Marina Bay Sands Hotel is one of the most luxurious hotels in Singapore, featuring an infinity pool blanketing the Central Business District (CBD) Area on the 57th floor. Stunning lasers shows are also held frequently which would put you in awe. Completed in 2010, the 3 towers facing the skyline is a go-to spot for visitors in order to view Singapore's town area in its full glory.

ArtScience Museum

Completed in 2011, the ArtScience Museum is the only museum in the world to bridge two cultural opposites, Arts and Science. It is Singapore's largest private museum, designed to be iconic and eco-friendly at the same time.

The lotus shaped museum serves as a distintive icon of Singapore's impressive architecture while funnelling rainwater through to the centre atrium of the building as a waterfall into a reflecting pool. The rainwater will then be used to power the building's restrooms. In addition, the roof can be transformed into an amphitheatre at night, enthralling audiences with light and laser shows and fireworks with the city skyline in the background.

Merlion Park

The Merlion, a reimagining of a mermaid and a lion, is an unmissable Singapore attraction. Half-fish and half-lion, the iconic Merlion resides at the waterfront Merlion Park. The body which resembles a fish, symbolises Singapore's humble beginnings as a fishing village, while its head represents Singapore's original name, Singapura, which means 'lion city' in Malay. Standing at 8.6 metres and weighing in at 70 tonnes, this iconic attraction must not be missed! Scattered around the island are other Merlion status - one in Sentosa, and another at Mount Faber. Can you find them?
