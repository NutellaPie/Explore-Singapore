National Gallery Singapore

About

The lastest addition into Singapore's art scene, the National Gallery Singapore has the world's largest collection of modern Singapore and Southeast Asian art. Situated in the heart of Singapore's city centre, the Gallery is housed within the City Hall and former Supreme Court which were redesigned into this award-winning venue.

The National Gallery Singapore reflects Singapore's unique heritage and geographical location. It provides insights into the unique art, heritage and history of the region. Working with international museums, the Gallery positions Singapore as a regional and international hub for visual arts through the presentation of Southeast Asian art in the global context.

Singapore and Southeast Asian art collection

Featuring a comprehensive representation of Singapore art, the Gallery showcases more than 8,000 pieces of art from Singapore's National Collection. It is one of the world's largest public collection of Singapore and Southeast Asian modern art and aims to provide a unique experience of art and inspire international appreciation of Singapore's rich history and heritage.

The National Gallery Singapore also provides a large variety of programmes such as guided tours, artist talks, conversations with curators and lecture series. Visitors will be able to gain deeper insights into the exhibition as well as experience the region’s very first dedicated art education centre, immersing themselves in a creative learning environment with a range of artful and active learning programmes and activities.
